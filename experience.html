Research Experience

Aug 2021 to Present

Purdue University · Energy and Transport Science Lab

Doctoral research · Advised by Prof. Partha P. Mukherjee, School of Mechanical Engineering

Physics-informed mathematical modeling and computation of electro-chemo-mechanical stability at electrode and electrolyte interfaces. Built a mesoscale kinetic Monte Carlo framework for void formation at the Li metal and solid electrolyte interface, coupled it with continuum transport and FFT-based micromechanics, and mapped the operating regimes that separate stable stripping from interfacial failure in solid-state batteries.

Aug 2020 to Apr 2021

Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur

Master's thesis · Advised by Prof. Monojit Chakraborty, co-advised by Prof. Partha P. Mukherjee (Purdue)

Investigated the mesoscale origin of voids in metal electrodes using kinetic Monte Carlo simulation, establishing the modeling foundation that became my doctoral research direction.

Aug 2019 to Apr 2020

Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur

Undergraduate thesis · Advised by Prof. Monojit Chakraborty and Prof. Sunando Dasgupta

Kinetic Monte Carlo modeling of nanoparticle drying patterns using the Metropolis algorithm in a lattice gas model, identifying the geometric patterns of phase separation. Awarded the best B.Tech undergraduate thesis in the Chemical Engineering department.

May 2019 to Jul 2019

École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)

Research internship · Laboratory of Fluid Mechanics and Instabilities, advised by Prof. François Gallaire

Studied the dynamics of a confined bubble under gravity and Marangoni actuation using finite element simulations in COMSOL, exploring how interfacial stresses drive bubble migration in confined geometries.

May 2018 to Jul 2018

Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research (JNCASR)

Research internship · Engineering Mechanics Unit, advised by Prof. Ganesh Subramanian

Analyzed the orientation dynamics of non-axisymmetric particles in various ambient planar linear flows, building intuition for suspension mechanics and low Reynolds number physics.
